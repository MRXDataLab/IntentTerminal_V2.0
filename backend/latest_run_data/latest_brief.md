#### 1. Market Context & Trigger (Business Background)
- **The Situation:** Kellogg's, despite its position as a masterbrand in India and robust distribution for its Corn Portfolio, is experiencing a consistent Year-over-Year (YoY) decline. This decline is evident in a failure to convert distribution into sustained off-takes and repeat purchases, specifically within Modern Trade and E-commerce channels. This situation is triggered by a notable migration of the consumer base toward Muesli and Granola, suggesting a loss of perceived relevance for traditional Cornflakes among modern consumers. Further exacerbating the issue is the market commoditization driven by local players, such as Mohan Cornflakes, offering similar products at significantly lower price points. Prior strategic interventions, including the high-decibel launch of Multigrain Cornflakes, packaging refreshes, varied pack sizes, and new functional claims, have not been sufficient to reverse this declining trend or counter the high competitive intensity.

#### 2. The Strategic Goal (Business Objective)
- **The Objective:** To understand and reverse the consistent Year-over-Year decline of Kellogg's Corn Portfolio in India. This research aims to specifically address the failure to convert robust distribution into sustained off-takes and repeat purchases within Modern Trade and E-commerce channels by deciphering why 'modern consumers' are shifting preferences towards Muesli and Granola, and how to counteract the commoditization by local players like Mohan Cornflakes. The ultimate goal is to identify effective strategies for repositioning, product innovation, and competitive response.
