### Strategic Research Brief: Kellogg’s India Corn Portfolio Behavioral Mapping

#### 1. Market Context & Trigger (Business Background)
**The Situation:** Kellogg’s India, while maintaining its status as an established masterbrand with robust distribution, is currently facing a year-on-year decline in its core Corn Portfolio. The primary catalyst for this research is the consistent decline in offtakes and the commercial failure of the Multigrain Cornflakes launch, which failed to achieve scale despite significant media investment. Previous tactical interventions, including packaging redesigns and updated functional claims, have failed to arrest this trend. There is a high-urgency requirement to diagnose the retention collapse, particularly within the East region, where market share is under acute pressure.

#### 2. The Strategic Goal (Business Objective)
**The Objective:** The objective of this research is to identify the friction points preventing high initial product trials from converting into sustained, habitual breakfast routines. This research unlocks the strategic decision of whether to reformulate the Multigrain offering, pivot brand positioning to reclaim "Category Relevance," or implement a defensive pricing/value strategy to counter local commoditization.

#### 3. Hypotheses to Stress-Test (Research Objectives)
*   **Hypothesis 1 (Demand Gravity):** The Multigrain health claims are failing to create a "sticky" breakfast ritual because the taste profile does not meet the sensory expectations of traditional core buyers, leading to one-time trials without adoption.
*   **Hypothesis 2 (Value Elasticity/Squeeze):** Local value players, specifically Mohan Cornflakes in the East, are successfully disrupting Kellogg’s brand pull by offering a "good enough" substitute at a lower price point, causing a migration of price-sensitive families.
*   **Hypothesis 3 (Choice Architecture):** Consumer breakfast preferences are shifting toward "premium-lite" formats; shoppers in Modern Trade are migrating away from the Cornflakes category entirely in favor of Muesli and Granola workarounds.

#### 4. Target Lens (Target Market / Audience)
**The Cohort:** The system will prioritize the digital footprints and sentiment of Modern Trade and E-commerce shoppers across India. Specific focus will be placed on the consumer segment within the **East region**, mapping their "Ritual Language" and the specific trade-offs they make during the morning meal occasion.

#### 5. The Search Perimeter (Scope: Geography, Timeline, Budget)
*   **Temporal Window:** Rolling 12-month trend analysis.
*   **Geography:** India (Primary focus: East Region).
*   **Product Scope:** The full Kellogg’s Corn Portfolio, including all SKU sizes and the Multigrain variant.

#### 6. The Threat Matrix (Competitive Context)
*   **Visible Rivals:** Mohan Cornflakes (Value leader), various Muesli and Granola brands (Premium disruptors).
*   **Invisible Threats:** The Outllyr discovery engine is authorized to scan for local "unbranded" or value-tier sellers in the East region that are emerging as substitute workarounds for price-conscious households.

#### 7. Internal Ground Truth (Existing Knowledge)
**Data Integration:** As no specific internal trial or repeat-purchase datasets were provided, the research will rely on the provided business baseline of YoY decline and media spend history. The AI will overlay external market signals against these known internal failure points (packaging/claim updates) to determine why previous interventions were ineffective.

#### 8. Action Confidence & Stop-Rules
*   **MSU (Marginal Signal Utility):** The AI will automatically halt data extraction when insights stabilize—meaning pulling additional data yields less than a 2% change in final sentiment or ritual mapping.
*   **Confidence Threshold:** Go/No-Go recommendations for product or positioning pivots will only trigger at an 85% convergence of signals across Modern Trade and E-commerce data points.

#### 9. Execution & Alignment
The data generated will be used jointly by the Marketing and Product teams for strategic positioning and operational adjustments to the Corn Portfolio.

3.  **The Decision Matrix:** A data-backed recommendation on how to resolve the Trial vs. Retention Paradox to stabilize the India Corn Portfolio.
